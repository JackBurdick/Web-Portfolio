Background

Childhood

I was born on the west coast but grew up in the rural midwest. Fishing, hunting, sports, and school consumed the majority of my time growing up.

U of M

I attended the University of Minnesota and completed a degree in biochemistry. I originally planned to apply to medical school, but I didn't feel passionate about the subject and found myself wishing I had been an engineer or programmer. After changing my mind, seemingly daily, on what I wanted to do post graduation I decided I was too invested in my biochem degree to start over. I finished my biochem degree on the off chance I would decided to pursue professional school again.

Career

After graduation, I quickly started my career in the life sciences. (Un)fortunately, I never did feel the urge to pursue professional school again and so I terminated my job and returned to school for what I truly enjoyed.

FAU

I am just finishing my prerequists for a masters in computer engineering. I enjoy the material far more than I previously did. When I studied biochemsitry, some material caught my attention but I was never able to "practice" biochemistry outside of school. Cloning, gene editing, viruses, prions, and transporter protiens are all wildly interesting to me, but since I wasn't able to clone anything in my kitchen (that I've disclosed anyway....) my excitement for the material would ____. Computer engineering is totally different. I am able to think of an idea and then, most importantly, I'm able to actually test it. I have numberous personal projects and in my free time am often reading textbooks "for fun".
